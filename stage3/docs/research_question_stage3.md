# Research questions – Stage 3 context

## Core research questions

- **RQ0.** How do large-scale circulation patterns differ between high-PM (p90) and low-PM (p10) days across the seasonal cycle?

- **RQ1.** Do monthly p90−p10 composites reveal consistent ridge–trough contrasts associated with polluted versus clean conditions?

- **RQ2.** How does the robustness of circulation contrasts vary by month, as measured by the spatial fraction of statistically significant differences?

- **RQ3.** Does the seasonal precipitation cycle help explain months with weak or absent synoptic separation?

- **RQ4.** Are PM₂.₅ and PM₁₀ influenced by the same large-scale circulation mechanisms, or do they exhibit different sensitivities across seasons?

---

## Rationale

Stage 3 extends the baseline framework established in Stage 2 by moving from:

- **event-only composites** → to **explicit contrasts between high and low pollution regimes**.

This approach isolates the circulation features that distinguish polluted from clean atmospheric states.

The stage also introduces precipitation diagnostics to provide a physical explanation for seasonal variability in synoptic control.

---

## Extreme-day framework

Extreme days are defined using **monthly percentile thresholds**:

- **p90:** high-PM days  
- **p10:** low-PM days

This definition:

- preserves the seasonal cycle,
- prevents mixing of wet- and dry-season dynamics,
- ensures comparable sampling across months.

Balanced composites are constructed using: N_used = min(N_high, N_low)


---

## Analytical structure

Stage 3 proceeds through four steps:

1. **Precipitation climatology**

   - monthly precipitation maps
   - Valley of Mexico rainfall time series
   - seasonal interpretation of wet scavenging and ventilation

2. **Baseline atmospheric structure**

   - monthly Z500 climatology
   - mean wind fields
   - definition of anomaly reference states

3. **Extreme circulation contrasts**

   - p90 composites
   - p10 composites
   - ΔZ500′ (p90 − p10) contrasts
   - statistical significance testing

4. **Air-quality exceedance diagnostics**

   - WHO exceedance frequency
   - exceedance magnitude
   - exceedance load

---

## Temporal and spatial scope

- **PM data**
  - Daily city-mean PM₁₀ and PM₂.₅
  - Period: 2012–2024

- **Atmospheric fields**
  - NARR reanalysis
  - 500 hPa geopotential height (Z500)
  - 500 hPa winds (U, V)

- **Precipitation**
  - NARR daily prate

- **Spatial domain**
  - Mexico-centered domain (12–33°N, 120–85°W)

---

## Limitations and scope boundaries

Stage 3 does **not** yet include:

- lagged circulation composites
- moisture transport diagnostics
- boundary-layer stability metrics
- objective circulation regime classification
- ENSO-phase stratification

These analyses are intentionally deferred to later stages to maintain methodological clarity.

Additionally:

- statistical significance highlights circulation contrasts but does not imply direct causality
- composite maps represent mean patterns, not individual events

---

## Intended contribution of Stage 3

Stage 3 provides:

- a physically interpretable contrast between polluted and clean atmospheric regimes,
- seasonal insight into when synoptic circulation strongly influences PM variability,
- and evidence that precipitation seasonality modulates synoptic control.

This stage therefore serves as the **diagnostic bridge** between the baseline composites of Stage 2 and the regime-oriented analyses planned for Stage 4.
